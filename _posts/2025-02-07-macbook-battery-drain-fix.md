Back in the day, you could just close your MacBook, unplug it, leave it for a week, then open it and notice nothing: battery charged, apps right where you left them.

Recently I noticed I can't even leave it overnight: 0% battery in the morning — what the hell?

Turns out there are some non-obvious settings you should turn off:

- **Power nap**: why do you need to check email and other crap while sleeping?
- **Screen time**: convenient if you want to observe yourself, but I never got any benefit from it;

(all of this is in System Settings, easiest to find through search)

If you want to maximize battery savings, turn off Wi-Fi and Bluetooth.

And for the nerds who want to dig deeper: [https://www.reddit.com/r/apple/comments/11nhs8a/macbook_battery_drain_while_in_sleep_actually/](https://www.reddit.com/r/apple/comments/11nhs8a/macbook_battery_drain_while_in_sleep_actually/)

Now I sleep peacefully, even my old Intel MacBook doesn't drain.
